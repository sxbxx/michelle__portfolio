Overview

The idea to create the youth awareness mobile app was pitched by Dr Peter Maulder and Russel Best.

I joined Youth Aware as a product designer when it was just a small team. We had three mobile developers and a product manager on board for the project.

They wanted to create two seperate mobile applications to help inform and educate the public about healthy eating and exercise.

I led the product, marketing, UX and UI strategy for the team. I've been extremely fortunate to have part of this journey and have grown tremendously during my time with the team of Youth Aware, some key achievements of which I have listed below:

Understanding the problem

The goals that the client is wanting the application to be a platform for every child to promote having an active lifestyle and sport performance by allowing to gain an understanding of how to make better health choices.

To engage such a young audience, it is needed to keep it relateable as possible.

Understanding the users

They are wanting to target secondary school students and their parents to help provide them the proper nutrition by providing them information and scenarios that will enhance them why it is essential of delivering the right nutrition.

Defining the MVP

At the client meeting were conducted workshops and sketching sessions with the team to map out the user flow and come up with a storyboard to capture the MVP.

    We identified the following key features that we wanted in the game application:

  • Minigames. Allows users to have a fun interactive element
  • Character Creation. It gives a personilised experiance for the users and has a character that they can relate to
  • Health/Nutrition Bar. An indication of the quality of the food
  • Onboarding. An onboarding experiance to welcome the user

Designs

With the clock ticking, I quickly did some mockups of the basic user journey before going over it with the desired styling. I opted for a refreshing gradient look and the simplicity of the UI. I then created for the developers a clickable prototype in Invision.

The Development Process

Gamification Mobile Application

As we went along with the project, the scope became larger, and the more unrealistic for everything became to be completed on time. Instead of focusing on all two apps that I have initially said, in the end, I just focused on making a gamification app since creating the interface and assets for it has a higher priority. Additionally, we had more ideas than we wanted to implement; however, we became short on time.

Also, due to the time limitations, I made some design choices to make a gender-neutral character model make it easier to build assets on top of it, and it can be reused between male/female character selections. Although I would of wanted to do more details with the assets, I feel like I have done a decent job with it.

Since I dont really do much illustration work, there was a bit of problem solving and experimentation of figuring out my own illustration style and is the most fitting for the context of the client/project. Additionally I had to do some research and problem solving on finding what is the best way of generating a large amount of assets in a easy format.
